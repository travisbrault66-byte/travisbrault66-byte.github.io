Readiness & Game Plan

Operational Readiness answers "how ready am I right now?" — it's a snapshot based on sleep, HRV, and recent shift load. Your Game Plan answers "what should I do today?" — it's based on how much cumulative stress your system has absorbed. Think of readiness as your current fuel level, and the Game Plan as the maintenance schedule.

Your Game Plan and your Operational Readiness score measure different things. Readiness reflects how you feel right now — based on last night's sleep and this morning's HRV. Your Game Plan looks at how hard your body has been working over the past week, using your shift strain and burnout trend. A week of heavy calls can push your Game Plan into recovery mode even on a day when your sleep and HRV are solid.

Yes, and it's actually common. A good night's sleep can lift your readiness score, but it doesn't erase a week of back-to-back busy shifts. FireWatch tracks both signals separately so one doesn't mask the other.

Three things: your shift strain score from recent calls, your 7-day burnout trend, and your 30-day burnout trend. If any one of those climbs high enough, your Game Plan shifts accordingly — regardless of how your readiness number looks that day.

When your system is significantly depleted — either a single shift with extreme strain, or a sustained stretch of heavy weeks that's pushed your burnout trend high. At that point FireWatch prioritizes recovery above everything else, regardless of how today's metrics look individually.

Devices & Data

FireWatch reads data from Apple Health (iOS) or Google Health Connect (Android). Open the app, go to the Connect Device screen, and tap "Connect Apple Health" or "Connect Health Connect." Your wearable must already be syncing to one of these platforms.

Any wearable that syncs to Apple Health (iOS) or Google Health Connect (Android) is supported. This includes Apple Watch, Garmin, Fitbit, Oura Ring, Polar, Whoop, and most other major fitness wearables. Note that some devices may not share all data types — see our connect screen for compatibility details.

Garmin does not natively sync HRV data to Apple Health or Google Health Connect. To get full HRV-based readiness scoring, download the free app Health Sync from the App Store or Play Store, which bridges your Garmin HRV data into Apple Health or Health Connect.

Account & Crew

Go to the Crew tab in the app. Tap "Create Crew" to start a new crew and get a shareable join code. Share that code with your crew members so they can join by tapping "Join Crew" and entering the code.

Subscriptions are managed through the Apple App Store or Google Play Store. To cancel, go to your device's subscription settings and cancel FireWatch. You will retain access to premium features until the end of your current billing period.

Go to the Profile tab → scroll to the bottom → tap "Delete Account." This permanently deletes all your data from our servers and your device. This action cannot be undone. You can also request deletion by emailing firewatchtravis@gmail.com.
